Arsenic Selectively Suppresses Nucleotide Excision Repair in African American Breast Cancer Stem-like Cells

Transcriptome-wide profiling of BRL-23 (NHW) and BRL-24 (AA) breast tissue-derived cell lines across parental and CD24−/CD49f+/CD44+ CSC-like populations under vehicle and chronic low-dose arsenic trioxide (0.3 μM, 14 days). BRL-23 and BRL-24 originate from non-diseased reduction-mammoplasty tissue; CSC-like populations are defined by the CD24−/CD49f+/CD44+ surface phenotype, not by tumor origin.

16,682 genes profiled 12 pairwise comparisons 44% transcriptome differs at baseline (7,355 DEGs) 48 GSEA runs • 4 MSigDB databases Primary finding: KEGG NER  NES = −2.75  FDR = 0.18 (C8)

Central Finding — Three-Condition Requirement

NER suppression in AA CSC-like cells requires ALL THREE simultaneously:

1 African American cellular biology
2 CSC-like identity (CD24−/CD49f+/CD44+)
3 Arsenic trioxide exposure (0.3 μM, 14 days)
Provenance: BRL-23 and BRL-24 were originally established and karyotyped in the Latimer laboratory (UPMC IRB 0609002). The 0.3 μM ATO LC10 dose, MWRI medium formulation, and 40 mM ATO stock protocol were previously characterized on the parental lines by Yousef (2025) and Alharbi (2025). The CD24−/CD49f+/CD44+ CSC-like flow sort and the transcriptomic comparisons reported here were performed in the present study and were not part of the prior published work, which analyzed unsorted parental cultures only.

📊 Baseline NER Context
At baseline (C1, AA vs NHW parental vehicle), individual NER genes including XPC and ERCC2 are actually higher in AA cells. However, the KEGG NER pathway score is not significant (NES = +0.98, FDR = 0.72) — meaning these individual gene differences do not reflect coordinated NER pathway activity. AA cells do not start with lower NER; the suppression emerges only under the combination of stem cell identity AND arsenic exposure.

The XPC Paradox — Race-Specific Arsenic Response

XPC is paradoxically induced by arsenic in AA parental cells (compensatory response) but suppressed in AA stem cells. NHW cells show no significant response at either level.

CDK7 — At the Intersection of NER and Cell Cycle Control

CDK7 is the catalytic kinase subunit of the TFIIH complex and therefore required for both Nucleotide Excision Repair (helicase loading) and RNA Pol II transcription initiation. It is also a cell cycle kinase (CDK-activating kinase, CAK). This dual role means CDK7 suppression in AA stem cells simultaneously impairs NER, transcription, and cell cycle checkpoints. CDK7 shows consistent downregulation in AA stem cells across C5–C8 (L2FC −0.39 to −0.61, FDR < 0.05), appearing in the C8 NER leading edge.

NER Role
TFIIH Kinase Subunit
Phosphorylates XPB (ERCC3) to open DNA helix at damage site. Without CDK7, TFIIH cannot load and the dual-incision reaction cannot initiate.
Stemness Role
CDK-Activating Kinase (CAK)
Activates CDK1, CDK2, CDK4, CDK6 by T-loop phosphorylation. Suppression impairs all major CDK functions — yet stem cells paradoxically maintain their proliferative signature (G2M NES = −2.47 in C8, suggesting mitotic stress not quiescence).
Clinical Relevance
Actionable Therapeutic Target
CDK7 inhibitors (THZ1, SY-1365) are in clinical trials. The co-suppression of CDK7 with other NER factors in AA stem cells may predict synthetic lethality with CDK7 inhibition in this cellular context.

Mechanistic Model — The Double Hit

Two converging molecular insults in AA breast CSC-like cells under arsenic: elevated oxidative DNA damage (from higher OxPhos/ROS baseline) combined with suppressed NER repair capacity (from arsenic-driven gene suppression).

Four Stable Themes — Integrative Summary

Theme 1: AA Elevated OxPhos in ALL 12 Comparisons
OxPhos NES values in racial comparisons: C1=+1.68, C2=+1.26, C11=+1.77, C12=+1.72. AA cells show higher mitochondrial oxidative metabolism in every racial comparison — generating excess ROS that makes NER suppression biologically consequential.
Theme 2: NHW Higher Inflammatory Signaling
Inflammatory NES (AA vs NHW, negative = higher in NHW): C1=−1.69, C11=−2.30, C12=−1.99. NHW cells show higher inflammatory baseline, amplified in stem cells. 23 core genes shared across C1 & C11 (ICAM1, IL1A, IL7R, LYN, TLR3 + 18 others). 18 additional genes gained exclusively in C11 stem cells (IL6, CXCL8, CCL2, CCL20, NFκB axis).
Theme 3: NHW Constitutively More Arsenic-Primed
KAN_RESPONSE_TO_ARSENIC_TRIOXIDE NES in racial comparisons: C1=−1.54, C11=−1.69, C12=−1.65 (negative = higher in NHW). NHW cells show higher baseline arsenic response signatures — suggesting they are pre-adapted to handle arsenic exposure.
★ Theme 4 (Primary): Three-Condition NER Suppression
Arsenic unmasks NER suppression specifically in AA stem cells. All three conditions are required: AA ethnicity + stem cell identity + arsenic exposure. NER is uniquely arsenic-dependent in the racial stem cell comparison (C11→C12).

Inflammatory Gene Panel & Racial OxPhos Baseline

Counterintuitively, NHW cells show consistently higher inflammatory tone. The core 7-gene signature is shared in parental (C1) and stem (C11) racial comparisons. AA cells show a stable OxPhos elevation across all conditions — the source of elevated ROS driving the double-hit mechanism.

The complete mechanistic story: why NER suppression is race-specific, arsenic-dependent, and stem-cell-restricted — told through four complementary visualizations.

C11 → C12: Arsenic as the NER Switch

NER is the ONLY major pathway transitioning from non-significant (C11, no arsenic) to significantly suppressed (C12, with arsenic) in the racial stem cell comparison. Arsenic is the decisive trigger.

ERCC1 — A Stem Cell NER Marker with Racial Amplification

ERCC1 is suppressed in all four stem-vs-parental comparisons (C5–C8). NHW suppression is modest and non-significant; AA suppression is significant in both C6 and C8, making ERCC1 a stem cell characteristic that is specifically amplified in AA cells — and the highest-confidence gene-level marker of race-specific NER disparity.

Pathway Story Browser

Five focused pathway story panels exploring the key secondary findings: oxidative metabolism, antioxidant response, self-renewal signaling, DNA repair switching, and within-stem racial differences.

Oxidative Phosphorylation — AA Baseline Elevation

OxPhos is consistently higher in AA cells (positive NES in racial comparisons C1, C2, C11, C12). This elevated mitochondrial activity generates excess ROS — the source of elevated DNA damage that makes NER suppression so consequential.

NRF2 Antioxidant Axis — Arsenic Induction & Racial Context

Key NRF2-target genes are induced by arsenic in parental cells (C3, C4) as a protective response, but this compensatory mechanism is blunted in stem cells (C9, C10) — particularly in AA stem cells where NER is simultaneously suppressed.

Self-Renewal Paradox — Stem Amplification Under Arsenic

While NER is suppressed, self-renewal pathways (Hedgehog, Notch, WNT, MYC, Hypoxia) are strongly amplified in AA stem cells under arsenic — creating a cell that is simultaneously more stem-like and less capable of repairing DNA damage.

BER vs NER — Racial Repair Pathway Divergence

In C7 (NHW Stem vs Parental Arsenic), REACTOME_BASE_EXCISION_REPAIR NES = −2.43, meaning BER is significantly elevated in NHW stem cells under arsenic. The paper's finding: NHW stem cells maintain BER while AA stem cells suppress NER — revealing a race-specific partitioning of DNA repair pathway vulnerability.

Within-Stem Arsenic Response — C9 (NHW) vs C10 (AA)

Comparing arsenic-vs-vehicle within NHW (C9) vs AA (C10) stem cells reveals the race-specific arsenic response. AA stem cells show selective NER suppression alongside amplified EMT and stemness signaling — a pattern absent in NHW.

Why DESeq2 + GSEAPreranked? — Head-to-Head Methods Comparison

The primary NER finding survives only under the DESeq2 + GSEAPreranked pipeline. Every alternative normalization and every alternative enrichment tool tested either flips the direction of key genes, inflates false positives, or dissolves the signal entirely. Numbers below are from the same 16 samples analyzed in parallel.

✓ Chosen pipeline

DESeq2 median-of-ratios + GSEAPreranked

Negative-binomial model on raw counts with empirical-Bayes dispersion shrinkage (apeglm). Supports the full factorial model ethnicity × Treatment × Type and borrows variance across genes, enabling principled inference even at n=1 stem cell conditions.

C8 KEGG NER: NES = −2.75, FDR = 0.18
✗ Fails on composition bias

CPM + LOG(avg2/avg1) fold change

CPM divides by total reads, so a single high-expressing gene (e.g. VIM at ~3,000–4,000 CPM) dominates the denominator differently in each line and inflates/deflates every other gene's apparent ratio. Also provides no statistical framework for n=1 stem cell conditions.

ALDH1A3 direction reversal
CPM: L2FC −2.92 (7.6× lower in AA)  vs.  DESeq2: +1.51 (2.9× higher)
Curated 54-gene CPM list contained zero NER pathway genes — primary finding structurally invisible.
✗ Collapses factorial design

Partek Flow built-in GSEA

Requires a two-level factor, so the four-variable design must be collapsed (AA vs NHW, Arsenic vs Vehicle, Stem vs Parental separately). This averages the AA-specific, arsenic-dependent NER signal into noise across racial backgrounds and treatments.

Stem vs Parental (DESeq2 counts): NES = −1.31, FDR = 0.38
Same comparison on CPM counts: NES = −0.56, FDR = 1.00
✗ Dilutes signal, can't do n=1

Partek Gene Set ANOVA

Weights every pathway gene equally (diluting the coherent core), requires within-group replicates (so C5–C12 stem comparisons are excluded), and can't generate leading-edge gene lists. The large "disruption" p-values confirm NER is perturbed but not where or in which direction.

DESeq2 NER main effect: AA vs NHW p = 0.80 • Arsenic vs Vehicle p = 0.49 • Stem vs Parental p = 0.011
CPM variant: AA vs NHW p = 0.049 — spurious signal at L2FC ≈ 0; 44% more pathways called significant across the three comparisons than DESeq2 Gene Set ANOVA, consistent with composition-bias false positives.

Bottom line

The race-specific, arsenic-dependent NER suppression signal requires four methodological choices in combination: (1) median-of-ratios normalization (not CPM) to neutralize composition bias, (2) empirical-Bayes dispersion shrinkage (not t-tests) to handle n=1 stem cell conditions, (3) a full factorial model (not collapsed two-group comparisons) to preserve the AA×Arsenic×Stem interaction, and (4) rank-weighted enrichment on pre-computed statistics (GSEAPreranked, not equal-weighted Gene Set ANOVA) to concentrate power on the sub-threshold NER genes that drive the signal.

Any one of those choices reverted to a simpler alternative erases the primary finding. The comparison above uses the same 16 samples across every pipeline, so the differences are purely methodological.

Study Limitations & Future Directions

Honest assessment of current study constraints and the five highest-priority validation experiments needed to extend these findings toward clinical translation.

Key limitation
Stem cell conditions sampled at n = 1 per group
Biological replicates for the four CD24−/CD49f+/CD44+ sorted stem cell groups (NHW Vehicle, NHW Arsenic, AA Vehicle, AA Arsenic) are n = 1 per group due to flow-sorting yield constraints. Stem-cell contrasts (C5–C12) therefore borrow variance via DESeq2 empirical-Bayes dispersion shrinkage from the n = 3 parental conditions rather than estimating it directly. The NER finding is statistically supported and directionally corroborated by two independent databases (KEGG and Reactome), but the three-way interaction (Race × Treatment × Cell Type) is not independently powered. Functional follow-up and additional stem-cell replicates are the primary validation requirements.
